Pomogranates

Back to Seasonal Crops

Season: Winter (December-February)

Growing Period: 90–120 days

Description: Pomegranates are well-suited for mild winter climates and grow best in temperatures between 60°F and 75°F. They prefer well-drained soil with a slightly acidic pH. A balanced fertilizer helps support fruit production. Pomegranates are typically ready to harvest in late fall or early winter, after about 6 to 8 months of growth.

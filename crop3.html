Carrot

Back to Seasonal Crops

Season: Spring (March-May)

Description: Carrots grow best in loose, sandy soil that allows their roots to develop properly. The soil should be free of rocks or hard clumps that might cause deformed roots. Carrots should be planted in early spring when the soil temperature is around 10–20°C. They need consistent moisture but should not be overwatered. Phosphate-rich fertilizers help in root development, and too much nitrogen should be avoided as it promotes leafy growth instead of strong roots. Carrots take about 60–80 days to mature, and they can be harvested when they reach the desired size. Keeping the soil moist helps prevent cracking in the roots.
